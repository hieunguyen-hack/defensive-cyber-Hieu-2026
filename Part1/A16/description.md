1. WA Government Microsoft 365 Data Breach (2026):

A Western Australian government audit found serious Microsoft 365 security misconfigurations across multiple agencies, which directly led to:
$71,000 being stolen (invoice fraud)
Exposure of sensitive data, including children’s information

Cause:
An attacker phished a senior employee’s Microsoft 365 account:
Weak MFA (e.g., SMS codes) allowed access
The attacker:
Registered their own device
Monitored emails for weeks
Sent fake invoices
This led to $71,000 being paid to the attacker
   
Source: https://www.cyberdaily.au/security/13308-wa-gov-microsoft-365-issues-led-to-71-000-being-stolen-and-exposure-of-child-data

2. University of Western Australia (UWA) Credential Breach:
The University of Western Australia (UWA) suffered a cybersecurity breach exposing thousands of passwords, forcing a campus-wide lockout and reset of all accounts.

Impact:
Systems access was temporarily shut down for all users
No evidence that data beyond passwords (e.g. personal info) was accessed
No signs of ransomware or attacker communication

Cause: The breach was caused by unauthorised access to a system storing user passwords
This implies a compromise of credential storage or authentication infrastructure, rather than individual phishing cases

Source: https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-08-11/university-of-western-australia-uwa-suffers-major-data-breach/105636074

3. WA Legal Practice Board Cyber Breach:
The Legal Practice Board of Western Australia (LPBWA) suffered a cyber attack (May 2025) involving unauthorised access to data, likely linked to a ransomware group, affecting legal practitioners’ information.

What happened:
On 21 May 2025, LPBWA detected unusual network activity and took systems offline
An unauthorised third party accessed data from its systems
A small amount of data was briefly leaked online, then removed within 24 hours

Impact:
Internal/operational information
Bank account details (Board + a small number of individuals)
Health information
Identity/personal data
Financial information 
-> all is leaked
Attack linked to a ransomware group (“Dire Wolf”)
Attackers claimed to have stolen large volumes of data (~300GB) and threatened to release it

Source: https://www.lpbwa.org.au/cyber-incident

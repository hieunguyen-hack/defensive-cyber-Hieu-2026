1. Stryker Global Network Disruption (March 2026):
Medical technology giant Stryker suffered a massive "Iran-linked" cyberattack that disrupted its global Microsoft environment. While initially thought to be a wiper attack by the group Handala, investigations revealed the attackers abused Microsoft Intune to run malicious commands.
The Impact: Global disruption to order processing, manufacturing, and shipping across dozens of countries.

source: https://www.stryker.com/us/en/about/news/2026/a-message-to-our-customers-03-2026.html

2. Companies House WebFiling security breach:

On 13 March, Companies House identified a flaw in its WebFiling service that allowed a logged-in user, under specific conditions, to access and potentially amend another company’s records. The service was swiftly shut down and remained offline for investigation until 16 March.
Data potentially exposed included directors’ dates of birth, residential addresses and company email addresses. There are concerns that it may also have been possible to submit unauthorised filings, such as changes to directors and submission of accounts.

3. LexisNexis Cloud Data Theft:

a high-profile breach targeting the legal and professional data giant's AWS infrastructure
What was Stolen?
The hackers exfiltrated roughly 2.04 GB of structured data, which included:
3.9 million records consisting of names, emails, phone numbers, and job roles.
21,042 enterprise accounts belonging to law firms, insurance companies, and universities.
118 .gov accounts specifically linked to U.S. government staff, federal judges, DOJ attorneys, and SEC personnel.
Cloud Infrastructure Data: 536 Redshift tables, 430+ VPC database tables, and 53 plaintext secrets from AWS Secrets Manager

source: https://www.dataminr.com/resources/intel-brief/lexis-nexis-breach-cloudinfrastructure/

4. Match Group / ShinyHunters SSO Breach (January/February 2026):

The parent company of Tinder, Hinge, and OkCupid fell victim to a sophisticated "vishing" (voice phishing) campaign by the ShinyHunters group.

The Impact: Over 10 million records allegedly stolen. The attackers bypassed Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) by impersonating IT staff to gain access to Okta Single Sign-On (SSO) credentials.

5. LA Metro Cyberattack (March 2026):

Los Angeles Metro suffered a cyberattack that disrupted internal systems and commuter services.
Hackers compromised internal IT systems, forcing shutdowns of administrative networks and affecting service-related systems.

Impact:
Disruption to payment systems and arrival information
Temporary shutdown of internal systems
No confirmed data theft

source: https://www.cyberdaily.au/security/13369-la-metro-shuts-down-employee-access-following-unauthorised-activity-on-network
